Who We Are

A Unified Platform Built on Trust and Transparency

CCW was established to address a critical gap in pediatric healthcare funding. Across the country, families facing catastrophic medical bills for their children often have no recourse when insurance, savings, and government schemes fall short. CCW closes that gap by creating a verified, audited pathway through which hospitals can submit genuine cases, donors can contribute with confidence, and government health authorities can oversee every transaction.

Unlike open crowdfunding platforms where authenticity is difficult to verify, every case on CCW begins with a hospital submission and undergoes a two-stage validation process. The hospital first confirms the medical details, estimated costs, and patient consent, and then a designated government health authority reviews and approves the case before it becomes visible to donors. This layered verification ensures that every rupee contributed reaches a genuinely verified need.

Our platform operates under the regulatory framework of the Department of Health and Family Welfare, with all fund disbursements routed directly to hospital accounts through audited banking channels. Donors receive 80G tax receipts, real-time impact updates, and full visibility into how their contributions are utilized, making CCW one of the most transparent healthcare funding initiatives in the country.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

The principles that guide every decision we make and every case we support.

Verification First

Every case is authenticated by a hospital and approved by a government authority before it reaches donors. Trust is never assumed, it is earned through process.

Radical Transparency

Donors can trace every rupee from contribution to disbursement. Full case histories, hospital details, and fund flow records are always accessible.

Child-Centered Care

Every decision prioritizes the child's medical needs and dignity. We partner only with hospitals that meet our pediatric care standards.

Collective Responsibility

We believe saving lives is a shared duty. Hospitals, donors, and authorities each carry a defined role in a single accountable system.

The Ecosystem

Three Stakeholders, One Mission

CCW unites three distinct groups, each with clear responsibilities that together create a closed loop of accountability.

Hospitals

Verified medical institutions submit patient cases with complete clinical documentation, estimated treatment costs, and consent records. They are the first line of authenticity, responsible for the accuracy of every case that enters the platform.

Donors

Individuals and organizations contribute financially to verified cases, choosing the children they wish to support. Donors receive tax receipts, progress updates, and a transparent record of how their contribution was applied to treatment.

Government Health Authority

Designated officials review and approve submitted cases, monitor fund disbursement, and conduct periodic audits. They serve as the independent verifier that gives every case on the platform its public credibility.

The Process

How CCW Saves Little Lives

From the moment a hospital identifies a child in need to the day treatment is completed, every step on CCW is designed to be verified, transparent, and accountable. Here is how the journey unfolds.

The Journey of a Case

Six Steps from Need to Treatment

Each case follows a structured pathway that ensures authenticity at every stage.

1

Hospital Identifies a Case

A verified hospital identifies a pediatric patient whose family cannot afford the required treatment. The medical team assesses the clinical need and prepares an estimated treatment plan with associated costs.

2

Case Registration on CCW

The hospital administrator submits the case through the CCW portal, entering patient details, treatment requirements, cost estimates, guardian consent, and uploading supporting medical documents for verification.

3

Government Authority Review

A designated health authority official reviews the submitted case, cross-checks the medical documentation, and either approves, requests clarification, or rejects the case based on verification standards.

4

Case Goes Live for Donors

Once approved, the case becomes visible on the donor portal with full transparency about the child's condition, treatment plan, hospital, and funding goal. Donors can review and choose which cases to support.

5

Donations Are Collected

Donors contribute securely through verified payment channels. Every contribution is recorded against the specific case, and donors receive instant confirmation along with an 80G tax receipt for their records.

6

Funds Disbursed & Treatment Begins

When the funding goal is reached, the collected amount is disbursed directly to the hospital's verified account through audited banking channels. The hospital begins treatment and provides progress updates to donors.

Shared Accountability

Responsibilities of Each Stakeholder

Clarity of roles is what makes the CCW model trustworthy. Each participant carries defined obligations.

Hospital Responsibilities

The hospital is responsible for accurate clinical documentation, honest cost estimation, obtaining guardian consent, submitting cases promptly, providing treatment updates, and maintaining records for audit. The hospital is the originator of every case and bears primary responsibility for its authenticity.

Donor Responsibilities

Donors are responsible for reviewing case details before contributing, donating through official channels only, and keeping their receipts for tax purposes. While donors choose which cases to support, they rely on the platform's verification to ensure their contributions reach genuine needs.

Authority Responsibilities

The government health authority is responsible for reviewing submitted cases against verification standards, approving or rejecting submissions, monitoring fund disbursement, and conducting periodic audits of hospital compliance and fund utilization across the platform.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from hospitals, donors, and authorities.

QHow does CCW verify that a case is genuine?
Every case undergoes a two-stage verification. First, the submitting hospital confirms the medical details and cost estimates with supporting documentation. Second, a designated government health authority independently reviews and approves the case before it becomes visible to donors. No case reaches the donor portal without both checks being completed.
QIs my donation tax deductible?
Yes. CCW is a registered entity under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. Every donation generates an automated 80G tax receipt that you can download from your donor dashboard and use when filing your annual tax return.
QHow long does the approval process take?
On average, a case moves from hospital submission to authority approval in two to three business days, provided all documentation is complete. Cases flagged for clarification may take longer, and the hospital is notified immediately of any additional requirements.
QWhat happens if a case does not reach its funding goal?
If a case does not reach its full goal within the specified timeframe, the collected funds are still disbursed to the hospital toward the treatment, and the case may be extended or partially fulfilled based on the hospital's assessment of what can be achieved with the available amount.
QCan a hospital register multiple cases at once?
Yes. Verified hospitals can register and manage multiple concurrent cases through their portal. Each case is tracked independently with its own status, documentation, and funding progress, while a unified dashboard provides an overview of all active submissions.
QHow are hospitals vetted to join the platform?
Hospitals must be recognized medical institutions with valid registration and pediatric care capabilities. They undergo an onboarding verification by the government health authority, which includes credential checks, infrastructure assessment, and a review of their financial controls before being granted submission access.

Built-In Protections

Safeguards That Protect Every Contribution

Multiple layers of verification and audit ensure that funds are used exactly as intended.

Two-Stage Verification

Every case is authenticated first by the submitting hospital and then independently approved by a government health authority before any donation is accepted.

Quarterly Audits

Government auditors review fund utilization, hospital compliance, and case documentation every quarter, with findings published in public audit reports.

Escrow Holding

Donations are held in audited escrow accounts until a case reaches its funding goal and the authority authorizes release, preventing premature or misuse of funds.

Full Traceability

Donors can trace every contribution from payment to hospital disbursement. Transaction records, case updates, and fund receipts are permanently accessible.
